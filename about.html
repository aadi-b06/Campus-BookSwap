About Campus BookSwap

We're on a mission to make textbooks more affordable and accessible for university students while promoting sustainability through reuse.

Our Mission

Campus BookSwap was founded with a simple yet powerful idea: to create a platform where university students can buy, sell, and swap textbooks directly with their peers, cutting out the middleman and saving money.

We believe that education should be accessible to all, and the high cost of textbooks should not be a barrier to learning. By facilitating peer-to-peer transactions, we're helping students reduce their educational expenses while promoting sustainability through the reuse of books.

Our platform is designed by students, for students, with a focus on creating a safe, user-friendly environment where campus communities can connect and help each other succeed academically.

Our Core Values

Affordability

We're committed to helping students save money on their educational materials by facilitating direct peer-to-peer transactions without markup or commission fees.

Sustainability

By encouraging the reuse of textbooks, we're reducing waste and promoting environmentally friendly practices within university communities.

Community

We foster connections between students on campus, creating opportunities for collaboration and support beyond just textbook exchanges.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Campus BookSwap work?

Campus BookSwap is a platform that connects university students who want to buy, sell, or swap textbooks. Sellers list their books with details and photos, and buyers can search for the books they need. Once a match is found, the platform facilitates communication between the two parties to arrange the exchange.

Is there a fee to use Campus BookSwap?

No, Campus BookSwap is completely free to use for all university students. We don't charge any listing fees, transaction fees, or commissions. Our goal is to help students save money, not add to their expenses.

How do I know if a book is in good condition?

Sellers are required to provide accurate information about the condition of their books, including any highlighting, notes, or damage. They also upload photos of the book. We encourage buyers to ask questions and inspect the book before completing the transaction. If a book's condition is misrepresented, you can report it to our support team.

How are payments handled?

Campus BookSwap does not handle payments directly. We recommend cash transactions when meeting in person, or using secure payment methods like Venmo, PayPal, or Zelle when necessary. Always exchange the book and payment simultaneously to ensure a fair transaction.

Is it safe to meet with other students?

We recommend meeting in public places on campus during daylight hours, such as the library, student union, or campus coffee shops. You can also bring a friend if you're concerned. Most universities have designated safe exchange zones that are monitored by campus security.
